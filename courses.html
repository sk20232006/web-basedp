In the event of an emergency, here's what you need to know to help.

First aid is about offering immediate assistance until professional help arrives. Follow these essential steps to feel more confident in providing help when someone is in need.

Essential First Aid Tips for Common Situations

  • Cuts and Wounds
  • Burns
  • Choking
  • Nosebleeds
  • Sprains and Strains
  • Poisoning
  • HeartAttack
  • Migraines

  1. Cuts and Wounds

    • Wash your hands to avoid infection.
    • Stop bleeding by applying firm pressure with a clean cloth.
    • Clean the wound with water; avoid using hydrogen peroxide or alcohol as they can delay healing.
    • Cover the wound with a sterile bandage.
    • Seek medical help if the wound is deep, won't stop bleeding, or shows signs of infection.

  2. Burns

    • For minor burns: Cool the burn with running cool (not ice-cold) water for 10-15 minutes.
    • Apply a sterile, non-stick bandage or clean cloth. Avoid popping blisters.
    • For severe burns: Do not apply water or remove clothing stuck to the skin. Cover with a clean cloth and seek medical attention immediately.
    • How to treat a first degree burn
    		3.


  3. Choking

    • Encourage the person to cough if they can breathe.
    • Perform the Heimlich maneuver:
      1. Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.
      2. Make a fist and place it above the navel.
      3. Thrust inward and upward forcefully until the object is expelled.
    • For infants, administer back blows and chest thrusts instead of abdominal thrusts.
    • How to deal with choking for adults

  4. Nosebleeds

    • Sit upright and lean slightly forward (not backward).
    • Pinch the soft part of the nose just below the bridge for 10-15 minutes.
    • Avoid blowing the nose for a few hours afterward.
    • Seek medical help if the bleeding doesnâ€™t stop after 20 minutes.

  5. Sprains and Strains (R.I.C.E Method)

    • Rest: Avoid using the injured area.
    • Ice: Apply an ice pack wrapped in a cloth for 15-20 minutes every 2-3 hours.
    • Compression: Use an elastic bandage to reduce swelling.
    • Elevation: Keep the injured area elevated above heart level.
    • Using the RICE Method for Injuries
    		6.


  6. Poisoning

    • Identify the poison and avoid inducing vomiting unless advised by a professional.
    • Call emergency services or a poison control center immediately.
    • If the person is unconscious, check for breathing and perform CPR if needed.
    • How to Treat Poisoning
    		7.


  7. Heart Attacks

    • Have the person sit and stay calm.
    • Call emergency services immediately.
    • If available, give aspirin (unless allergic) to help thin the blood.
    • Perform CPR if the person becomes unconscious and stops breathing.
    • How to deal with HeartAttacks

  8. Migraines

      1. Move to a quiet, dark room:
        • Bright lights and noise can worsen symptoms. Resting in a calm environment can help.
      2. Apply a cold compress:
        • Place a cold pack or a damp cloth on your forehead or the back of your neck to ease the pain.
      3. Stay hydrated:
        • Dehydration can trigger or worsen migraines. Sip water slowly.
      4. Over-the-counter pain relief:
        • Medications like ibuprofen or acetaminophen can help if taken early in the attack.
        • Avoid overuse to prevent rebound headaches.
      5. Manage nausea:
        • Ginger tea or peppermint can help alleviate nausea, a common migraine symptom.
      6. Caffeine:
        • Small amounts of caffeine may help relieve early migraine symptoms but avoid overconsumption.
      7. Relaxation techniques:
        • Deep breathing, meditation, or light stretching can reduce muscle tension and stress.
